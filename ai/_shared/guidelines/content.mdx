# Content

Great Learning's editorial system. These rules govern every word that ships on a GL surface — product UI, course pages, marketing pages, in-app notifications, error messages. The voice stays constant; tone shifts with surface. Casing, punctuation, sentence length, and component-specific copy rules are all measured.

> Use the companion review workflow (`gl-content-review` skill) to audit drafts. This page is the reference; the skill is the procedure.

## Platform priorities

| Platform | Key content expectations |
|---|---|
| **Olympus** | Neutral, functional UI copy. Clarity over personality. No marketing language inside the product. Tone is consistent regardless of emotion — never exclamatory, never robotic. Student-facing and faculty-facing copy must be written separately; never use the same label for both user types where context or action differs. See **Olympus rules** below. |
| **Great Learning Academy — Pro+** | Subscription-value framing. Emphasise breadth of access, flexibility, and self-paced learning. Tone is encouraging and practical. CTAs drive subscription sign-up or free trial activation. Avoid course-specific language; focus on the library and experience. |
| **Great Learning Academy — Certificate course** | Outcome and credibility-focused. Hierarchy: course overview → curriculum → instructor → certificate → CTA. Earn trust quickly — learners are paying for a specific credential. Avoid duplication across sections. **SEO density rules apply.** |
| **University Degree & Certificate Programs** | Trust and credibility-building. University partner name and reputation must be prominent. Hierarchy: program overview → curriculum → faculty → outcomes → admission. Formal but accessible — not cold. **SEO density rules apply.** |
| **Enterprise** | B2B tone — clear, professional, outcome-oriented. Lead with business impact. Address L&D buyers, not learners directly. ROI, scalability, and customisation are key value propositions. Avoid consumer-facing language like "your career" or "get hired". |
| **Marketing & Brand** | Campaign-aware. Must align with the active campaign tone. Lead with emotional hook for awareness; lead with outcome for lead-gen. Brand voice consistent with GL's core identity. |

## Audience messaging

| Audience | Lead messaging priority | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| **College students** | Employability, first job, skill-building, certification recognition, peer community | Corporate or inaccessible tone; assuming prior work experience |
| **Early-career professionals** | Career acceleration, upskilling, job-switching, certification for promotion | Senior decision-making framing; aspirational language without tactical grounding |
| **Mid-career professionals** | Career transition, leadership readiness, specialisation, ROI on time invested | Entry-level framing; oversimplified outcomes; anything that feels "basic" |
| **Senior professionals** | Transformation, executive credibility, peer network, long-term career strategy | Tactical or skill-list-heavy copy; anything that feels like training rather than development |

## Casing

| Surface | Rule |
|---|---|
| Banner headers | Sentence case (unless the full header is a product/program/brand name → Title Case) |
| CTA buttons | Title Case (`Enrol For Free`, `Get Started Today`) |
| Navigation items | Title Case |
| Form labels | Title Case |
| Tooltips | Sentence case |
| Error messages | Sentence case |
| Body copy | Sentence case |
| Main headings | Title Case |
| Subheadings | Sentence case |

**Always capitalise**: course names, university names, product names, proper nouns.

**Subtext / descriptions**: Sentence case throughout. Capitalise course names, universities, product names only.

**Slash rule**: If the word before a slash is capitalised, capitalise the word after it too. ✅ `Programs/Courses`

## Grammar

- Subject-verb agreement.
- Consistent tense — do not mix past and present.
- **Active voice required.** Flag passive constructions.
  - ✅ "The course deadline is in 2 weeks"
  - ❌ "In 2 weeks, there will be a course deadline"
- Avoid first and second person in the same sentence. Prefer second person.
  - ❌ "Great Learning benefits that we love"
  - ✅ "Why engineers love our programs?"
- Do not bury the objective. Lead with it.
  - ✅ "To sign up for our course, click here"

## Punctuation & symbols

- **Full stops**: Not used in solitary sentences, CTAs, labels, or button text. ✅ `Sign up`  ❌ `Sign up.`
- **Oxford comma**: Required. ✅ "students, professionals, and business owners"
- **Colons after labels**: Not used.
- **Ampersand (`&`)**: Headings only. Do not repeat in subtext.
- **Hyphen (`-`)**: Use moderately to conjoin words (`decision-making`, `real-world`). Avoid in main headings for SEO.
- **En dash (`–`)**: Number ranges only. `4:00 PM–5:00 PM`
- **Em dash (`—`)**: In place of parentheses. `not applicable — unless the student decides to leave —`
- **Ellipses (`…`)**: Rarely used. Flag if overused.
- **Rupee symbol**: Use `₹` — not `INR`, `Rs`, or `Rupees`.
- **Parentheses `()`**: Do not use in main copy.
- **Guiding symbols** (arrows, circles): Not used.
- **Incomplete signifiers**: Flag and remove `etc.`, `and more`, `among others` — they lose clarity.

## Voice & tone

GL's voice is **confident, cordial, motivational, human, and concise**. Tone shifts by context — but voice stays constant.

- **Confidence**: assured and credible.
- **Human warmth**: speak directly to the user as a person — "talk to the user as though they're standing right in front of you".
- **Motivation without patronising**: urge action without overwhelming or condescending.
- **Optimism and aspiration**: highlight positive impact on the learner's career.
- **Social proof**: use numbers and outcomes. ✅ "8,000 graduates completed the data science course in 12 months"
- **No jargon or slang** in main page/app communication (first fold of all pages/apps).
- **No plagiarism** from other edtech sites.
- **No ambiguity in dialog questions** — be direct about what action is being confirmed.
  - ❌ "Complete payment?"
  - ✅ "Do you wish to proceed?"

## Length & layout

| Element | Max length |
|---|---|
| Title / Header | 6 words |
| Subtext | 120 characters (1–2 lines max below a heading) |
| Button / CTA | 3 words |
| Pop-up title | 3 words |
| Testimonials | 100 words |
| Body sentences | 30 words max (app/desktop) |

Flag: unnecessarily long sentences, redundancy, content that does not fit mobile-first layouts. Goal: brevity + readability + conversion focus.

## Component-specific rules

### CTAs / Buttons
- Short, commanding sentences: `Create Account`, `Find Your Course`.
- Use `Log In` not `Login`; `Sign Up` not `Signup` — separate the words.
- No punctuation in CTAs.
- All CTAs in Title Case — including main CTAs. ✅ `Enrol For Free`  ❌ `ENROL FOR FREE`  ❌ `Enrol for free`

### Error messages
- State what happened and why.
- Offer a next course of action.
- Non-condescending tone.
  - ❌ "Looks like you made a mistake. Try again."
  - ✅ "There seems to be an issue. Shall we give it another try?"

### Empty states
- Never leave blank — explain why the screen is empty.
- ✅ "Track progress once learner begins activity"
- Do not prompt the user to leave or close the page.

### Pop-up modals
- Short, crisp, deadline-oriented.
- Title Case or ALL CAPS + numbers to grab attention.
- ✅ "Get 10% off on your first course"

### Thank-you screens
- Friendly, celebratory.
- Use "thank you", "please", "we hope".
- Formal and assuring, not purely transactional.
  - ✅ "We hope our brochure helps. Please check your inbox."

### Dialogs — date & time format
- **International**: `12 May, 2021` (no superscript).
- **Domestic**: `12th May, 2021` (with superscript).
- **Time**: 12-hour clock with AM/PM. Include timezone: `CDT`, `BST`, `IST`, etc.

## Consistency

- Consistent terminology across the page/screen.
- Consistent product and course naming conventions.
- Consistent capitalisation of brand/product terms.
- No repetition of words across header → subtext → CTA in the same unit.

## SEO — keyword density (program & course pages)

> Applies to all program pages, course pages, and landing pages. Skip for Olympus, app UI, and offline/print marketing.

Google treats keyword stuffing as a spam policy violation. The goal is natural integration, not avoidance.

### The threshold rule

GL follows Google's recommended density of **1–2% of total page word count** as the upper bound for any single primary keyword or exact-match keyword phrase. Count covers the entire page — headings, body, CTAs, FAQ questions and answers, and visible metadata (`<title>`, meta description).

- 600-word course page: primary keyword **no more than 6–12 times**
- 1,000-word course page: primary keyword **no more than 10–20 times**
- Density **above 2%** must be flagged
- Density **above 5%** is a critical violation — mandatory revision before publish

### Required keyword placements

The primary keyword must appear exactly once in each of these high-value positions. These are non-negotiable for SEO and **do not count toward the density threshold**:

- Page `<title>` tag
- Meta description
- H1 (page or course title)
- First 100 words of body copy
- At least one H2 or H3 subheading

Beyond these placements, every further appearance must arise naturally. Flag pages where the keyword is **absent** from any of the above — under-optimisation is equally worth correcting.

### What to flag

- Primary keyword density **exceeds 2%** of total page word count.
- Density **exceeds 5%** — critical, mandatory revision.
- Exact keyword phrase in **consecutive sentences**.
- Keyword in **both a heading and its immediately following subtext** without meaningful variation.
- Keyword **more than twice within a single section** (hero, about, FAQ, curriculum, etc.) — even if total page density is within range.
- Keyword in a CTA where it reads unnaturally. ❌ `Enrol in our Python for Data Science and Machine Learning course today` → ✅ `Start Learning Today`
- A **FAQ question and its answer both open with the exact keyword phrase**.
- Keyword in **image alt text, aria labels, or button labels** purely for SEO with no functional purpose.

### Semantic variation (required practice)

Google understands synonyms and contextually related terms. Repeating the exact phrase beyond the required placements is unnecessary and harmful.

| Primary keyword | Acceptable variants |
|---|---|
| `Python for Data Science` | `this course`, `the program`, `data analysis with Python`, `machine learning in Python` |
| `Data Science and Machine Learning` | `ML fundamentals`, `predictive modeling`, `this curriculum` |
| `Artificial Intelligence course` | `this program`, `the course`, `your AI learning path` |
| `Cybersecurity certification` | `the program`, `this certification`, `IT security training` |
| `Cloud Computing course` | `the course`, `this program`, `cloud training` |

### Exemptions

Not counted toward density, never flagged as repetition:

- Course module titles within the curriculum section (proper content descriptors, not promotional copy).
- Structured data / schema markup (not visible to users).
- Navigation breadcrumbs.
- Instructor names and credentials.

### Severity reference

| Scenario | Severity | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Keyword density 2–5% | ⚠️ Medium | Flag, suggest variants |
| Keyword density above 5% | 🔴 Critical | Mandatory revision before publish |
| Keyword in consecutive sentences | ⚠️ Medium | Flag, rewrite affected sentences |
| Keyword in both FAQ question and answer opening | ⚠️ Medium | Flag, rewrite answer opening |
| CTA contains full keyword phrase unnaturally | ⚠️ Medium | Rewrite CTA |
| Keyword missing from H1 or first 100 words | ⚠️ Medium | Flag as under-optimised |
| Keyword missing from meta description or page title | ⚠️ Medium | Flag as under-optimised |

## Olympus rules

> Applies only to copy that ships inside Olympus.

Olympus is a blended LMS serving both students and faculty. Copy must be neutral, functional, and unambiguous. It helps users complete tasks — it does not inspire or sell.

### Voice & tone on Olympus

- **Neutral and functional.** Copy informs and guides. It does not motivate, celebrate, or market.
- **No exclamation marks** anywhere on the platform.
- **No marketing language**. Avoid "unlock your potential", "transform your career", "world-class". Those belong on landing pages.
- **No filler phrases**. Remove "please note", "kindly", "as you may know", "feel free to".
- **Direct address.** Use "you" / "your" for students. Use "you" for faculty only where the action is unambiguous.
- **Consistent register.** Tone must not vary between screens — a progress dashboard and an error message should feel like they come from the same system.

### Student vs faculty copy

Olympus serves two distinct user types. **Never reuse the same label or CTA for both when context or permission level differs.**

| Surface | Student label | Faculty label |
|---|---|---|
| Submission action | `Submit assignment` | `Review submissions` |
| Course access | `Continue learning` | `Manage course` |
| Session action | `Join session` | `Start session` |
| Progress view | `Your progress` | `Learner progress` |
| Notification sender | — | `Your faculty` or faculty name |

Flag any copy that uses a generic label (e.g. `View course`, `Open session`) without confirming whether it's student-facing, faculty-facing, or shared. If shared, the label must work accurately for both contexts.

### Course cards & catalogues
- Title: course or program name only — no taglines or descriptors on the card itself.
- Metadata order: Duration → Format → Level (if shown).
- One CTA per card. Student: `Continue` or `Start`. Faculty: `Manage` or `View`.
- Do not truncate course names mid-word. Use ellipsis (`…`) only if engineering enforces a character limit.

### Progress tracking & dashboards
- Use completion language, not percentages. ✅ "3 of 8 modules complete"  ❌ "37.5% done"
- No motivational language on progress bars or completion states. ❌ "You're crushing it!"  ✅ "Module 3 complete"
- Empty dashboard state explains what will populate the view. ✅ "Your progress will appear here once you begin a module."
- Faculty dashboard uses learner-count language. ✅ "24 learners enrolled"  ❌ "24 students"

### Notifications & alerts
- Lead with the action or deadline, not the sender. ✅ "Assignment due 15 June"  ❌ "Your faculty has set an assignment due 15 June"
- Include a timestamp or deadline in every notification where one is relevant.
- No ALL CAPS for urgency — use deadline proximity instead. ✅ "Due tomorrow, 15 June"  ❌ "URGENT: Assignment due"
- Faculty notifications to learners: sender attribution goes at the end. ✅ "Session starts at 4:00 PM today — Data Science: Module 4 | Prof. Rajan"

### Assignments & submissions

Status labels must be unambiguous and consistent:

| Status | Student label | Faculty label |
|---|---|---|
| Not yet started | `Not started` | `Awaiting submission` |
| In progress | `In progress` | `In progress` |
| Submitted | `Submitted` | `Pending review` |
| Reviewed | `Reviewed` | `Reviewed` |
| Overdue | `Overdue` | `Overdue` |

- Do not use `Pending` for student-facing status — it is ambiguous (pending on whom?).
- Deadline display: always date + time + timezone. ✅ "Due 15 June, 11:59 PM IST"

### Error & empty states
- Every error answers three questions: What happened? Why? What should the user do next?
  - ✅ "We couldn't load your assignments. Check your connection and try again."
  - ❌ "Something went wrong."
- Empty states must never be blank. Always explain what will appear and when.
  - ✅ "No assignments yet. Your faculty will add assignments here once the course begins."
  - ❌ "No assignments found."
- Do not use "Oops", "Uh oh", or informal openers — Olympus tone is neutral, not conversational.

### Navigation & menus
- All nav items: Title Case.
- Labels are task-oriented, not section-oriented. ✅ `My Courses`  ❌ `Courses Section`
- Faculty nav items must clearly signal scope. ✅ `Manage Learners`  ❌ `Learners`
- Avoid abbreviations in nav unless space is critically constrained and the short form is universally understood (`AI/ML` is acceptable; `Mgmt` is not).

### Live session / webinar UI
- Pre-session: show countdown with timezone. ✅ "Starting in 20 minutes — 4:00 PM IST"
- In-session labels use present-tense actions. ✅ `Leave session`  ❌ `Exit`
- Faculty in-session controls use faculty-specific language. ✅ `End session for all`  ❌ `End session`
- Post-session: confirm recording availability if applicable. ✅ "Recording will be available within 24 hours."

### Always flag on Olympus
- Marketing or aspirational language inside any product screen.
- Exclamation marks anywhere.
- Generic labels used for both student and faculty without verification.
- Error messages that don't explain cause and next step.
- Empty states with no explanatory copy.
- Percentage-based progress language.
- Notification copy that leads with the sender instead of the action/deadline.
- Assignment status labels that differ from the standard set above.
- Truncated course names without ellipsis enforcement.
- Missing timezone on any time-based UI element.

## Review workflow

When auditing a piece of content, run the `gl-content-review` skill. It walks through:

1. **Mode** — content review, new page creation, or other.
2. **Intake** — platform, audience, intent.
3. **Checklist** — every section above, scoped to the surface in question.
4. **Output** — annotated review with original / issue / suggested fix per flag, plus a final verdict (Approved / Needs Revision / Major Rework Required).

Default to **UK English** when the region is undeterminable and flag the assumption.
